About Formula 1

the Premier Global Motorsport

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel, single-seater cars. It is sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile. The F1 World Constructor's; and Driver's Championships have been sought after by teams and drivers since 1950.

F1 seasons are yearly with around 20 races a year called Grand Prix. Races are split into practice sessions on Fridays, qualifying sessions on Saturdays, and the Grand Prix itself on Sundays. There are 10 teams, 2 drivers per team.

F1 cars are the fasted regulated road-course cars in the world and are immensely high-tech. For years, F1 has pushed the boundaries of automotive science.
