Formula One Teams

The 2022 Formula 1 Teams

Oracle Red Bull Racing

One of two teams owned by Red Bull. Honda-powered, Austrian license, UK-based
Constructors champions: 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013

Mercedes AMG Petronas

Owned by Mercedes-Benz. Engine manufacturer for other teams. German license, UK-based
Constructors Champions: 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021

Scuderia Ferrari

Owned by Ferrari. Oldest surviving and most successful F1 team. Engine manufacturer. Italian license and base.
Constructors Champions: 1961, 1964, 1975-77, '79, '82-'83, '99-'04, 07, 08

Mclaren

Owned by Mclaren Auto. Same pedigree as Ferrari. Mercedes-powered. UK license and base.
Constructors champions: 1974, 1984, 1985, 1988, 1989, 1990, 1991, 1998

BWT Alpine

Owned by Renault. Rebranded from legendary Renault to promote sportcar devision. Renault-powered. French license and UK-based.
Constructors champions: 2005, 2006

Scuderia AlphaTauri

Owned by Red Bull. Sister team for Red Bull. Usually for junior drivers. Honda-powered. Italian license and base.
Constructors champions: None

Aston Martin Cognizant

Formerly Spyker/Force India/Racing Point. Midfield team. Mercedes-powered. UK license and base
Constructors champions: None

Alfa Romeo Orlen

Formerly Sauber. Midfield team. Junior drivers from here are promoted to Ferrari. Ferrari-powered. Italian license, Switzerland-based
Constructors champions: None

Williams

Same legendary pedigree and history as Mclaren and Ferrari. Junior drivers from here are promoted to Mercedes. Mercedes-powered. UK license and base
Constructors champions: 1980, 1981, 1986, 1987, 1992, 1993, 1994, 1996, 1997

Uralkali Haas

Only American team. Recent addition (from 2016). Used to be midfield, but now lower in the pack. Ferrari-powered. US and UK based. American license
Constructors champions: None
